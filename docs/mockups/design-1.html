France Plans National Migration from Windows to Linux Across Government Systems

France's DINUM is preparing a national strategy to replace Windows with Linux on government workstations, citing digital sovereignty and reduced dependency on non-European vendors.

France DINUM Linux

France's digital agency, DINUM, has published an internal roadmap for replacing Windows across approximately 280,000 government workstations with a Linux-based operating system. The initiative, codenamed Open-Interop, targets completion by 2028.

The move is driven by concerns over data sovereignty following the CLOUD Act, which allows US authorities to compel American companies to provide access to data stored anywhere in the world, including on European government servers.

Initial pilots will run across Ministry of Finance offices beginning Q3 2026. The document also outlines training programmes for civil servants and a dedicated migration support team.
