France Plans National Migration from Windows to Linux Across All Government Systems Tech Heise 6h 3m
France DINUM Linux Open-Interop

France's DINUM has published an internal roadmap for replacing Windows across approximately 280,000 government workstations with a Linux-based operating system, codenamed Open-Interop, targeting completion by 2028.

The move addresses data sovereignty concerns under the CLOUD Act, which compels American companies to share data stored anywhere globally, including European government servers.

Initial pilots run across Ministry of Finance offices beginning Q3 2026, with civil servant training programmes and a dedicated migration team.
