France Plans National Migration from Windows to Linux Across All Government Systems by 2028

France's DINUM has published the Open-Interop roadmap to move 280,000 government workstations to Linux, citing data sovereignty concerns and CLOUD Act compliance risks.

France DINUM Linux Open-Interop

France's digital agency, DINUM, has published an internal roadmap for replacing Windows across approximately 280,000 government workstations with a Linux-based operating system. The initiative, codenamed Open-Interop, targets completion by 2028.

The strategic shift is driven by data sovereignty concerns under the US CLOUD Act, which compels American technology companies to provide access to data stored anywhere globally — including on European government infrastructure.

Initial pilots will run across Ministry of Finance offices beginning Q3 2026. The programme includes dedicated civil servant training and a government-wide migration support team.
